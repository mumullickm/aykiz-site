Probashi.

A guide for Bangladeshi workers heading to Saudi Arabia.

halal-income product

Probashi is a one-time guide bundle (PDF plus audio) covering the practical realities of moving from Bangladesh to KSA for work. Sold in BDT via bKash. Built for the people who actually take this journey, not the agents who profit from it.

the problem

Information asymmetry kills migrants.

The Bangladesh-to-KSA labor migration is one of the largest corridors in the world. Hundreds of thousands of workers leave Bangladesh for Saudi Arabia every year. The information they need (visa types, recruitment agency red flags, what to sign, what to never sign, salary expectations by sector, how to send money home without losing 8 percent to bad channels) is scattered across Facebook groups, predatory agent offices, and rumor.

Predatory recruitment is a real problem. So is the silence of family members who already made the journey and do not want to admit how hard the first six months were.

the product

A guide that respects the reader.

Format

A PDF and a companion audio narration in Bangla. One purchase. Lifetime access. Updates pushed when KSA labor law or visa categories change. No subscription. No email funnel.

Distribution

Sold via a bKash-gated download page. Bangla-first. Phone-first. No credit card required. Companion free Android app for the navigation and quick-reference layer.

Scope

License-safe. I am not an immigration consultant. The product is plain-language information from public sources, my own research, and interviews with returnees. It does not replace legal advice.

Pricing

Priced low enough that a worker preparing to migrate can afford it without losing a day's wage. The point is not the unit economics. The point is the audience can actually buy it.

why I am building this

Skin in the game.

My family will eventually navigate KSA paperwork. I want a Madinah-area marketing role in the next three years. Building this product is partly research, partly portfolio, and partly the right thing to do for the corridor I came from.

Probashi sits next to Wasilah in my work for the same reason: both are designed for Bengali Muslim audiences that the rest of the market keeps treating as an afterthought.
